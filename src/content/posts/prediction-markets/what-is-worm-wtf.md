# How Worm.wtf Works (Simple Explanation)

At its core, Worm.wtf is a binary prediction market. Events are framed as yes/no questions, like "Will Bitcoin hit $150,000 by March 2026?" or "Will Taylor Swift announce a new album before summer?" Shares trade between near zero and $1, with the price reflecting the crowd's perceived probability.

You buy Yes if you think it will happen, No if you think it will not. If you are right at resolution, your shares settle at $1 each. Wrong? They go to zero. No house edge, just peer-to-peer trading.

What sets it apart is the ease of creation. Describe your idea in plain English to the Worm AI (or tag their bot on X), and it generates a market with clear rules, sources for resolution, and a fair 50/50 start. Markets use UMA's optimistic oracle for settlement once volume hits around $200, keeping things decentralized and trustworthy.

Add in pre-sales with bonding curves for early liquidity (zero loss if no one trades), limit orders, and leveraged positions up to 3x or more, and you have a platform built for rapid, viral growth.
