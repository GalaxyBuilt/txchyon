## Introduction to Tangem Hardware Wallet Setup

Tangem stands out in 2026 as one of the most user-friendly hardware wallets. Its credit-card form factor uses NFC technology—no batteries, cables, or charging required. Unlike traditional hardware wallets with seed phrases, Tangem offers optional multi-card backups and private key generation directly on the chip.

This guide provides a complete step-by-step walkthrough for setting up a new Tangem wallet, whether you bought a single card or a pack of 2–3 for backups. Perfect for beginners seeking simplicity combined with strong security.

For broader hardware wallet comparisons, see our [best hardware wallets for 2026](/blog/security-privacy/best-hardware-wallets-2026). New to crypto? Start with our [crypto terminology glossary](/blog/getting-started/crypto-terminology-glossary).

## Why Choose Tangem

Key advantages:
- Extremely portable and durable (water-resistant cards)
- No seed phrase needed (optional access codes instead)
- EAL6+ certified chip (same level as passports)
- Supports thousands of cryptocurrencies
- Fast NFC tap-to-use with iOS and Android

Tangem is ideal for users who want hardware-level security without the complexity of traditional seed phrases.

## What You Need Before Starting

- A Tangem card or pack (buy only from official sources)
- Smartphone with NFC enabled (most modern iPhones and Androids)
- The official Tangem app (download from App Store or Google Play)

## Step-by-Step Tangem Wallet Setup Guide

### Step 1: Unbox and Verify Authenticity

Open your Tangem package. Genuine cards include:
- Holographic sticker and serial number
- QR code for authenticity check

Scan the QR code or visit the Tangem website to confirm the card is original. Never buy second-hand or unsealed cards.

### Step 2: Download and Install the Tangem App

Search "Tangem" in the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android). Install the official app developed by Tangem Wallet.

Enable NFC on your phone:
- iPhone: NFC is always on for compatible models (iPhone 7 and newer)
- Android: Go to Settings > Connections > NFC and turn it on

### Step 3: Create Your New Wallet

Open the Tangem app and select "Create wallet" or "Scan card."

Tap your primary Tangem card on the back of your phone. The app will detect it via NFC and begin the setup process.

The private key is generated entirely on the card's secure chip—never exposed to the phone or internet.

### Step 4: Set Up Access Code (Highly Recommended)

Tangem allows an optional access code (like a PIN) for extra protection.

Choose a strong 6–12 digit code and confirm it. This code will be required every time you use the card.

If you forget the code, you can still recover using backup cards (see below).

### Step 5: Add Backup Cards (Recommended for Packs)

If you purchased a 2- or 3-card pack:
- Tap "Add backup" in the app
- Scan each additional card one by one
- The app duplicates the private key securely across cards

With backups, losing one card does not mean losing your funds—as long as you have at least one remaining card and the access code.

Store backup cards in separate secure locations (safe, bank deposit box, trusted family).

### Step 6: Verify Wallet Creation

The app will show your new wallet address for major coins (BTC, ETH, etc.). You can now view balances (empty at first) and supported networks.

Optionally enable biometric unlock (Face ID/Touch ID/fingerprint) for convenience.

### Step 7: Receive Your First Crypto

To test:
- Copy a receive address (e.g., Bitcoin or Ethereum)
- Send a small amount from an exchange—see our [how to buy crypto guide](/blog/getting-started/how-to-buy-crypto)
- Confirm receipt in the Tangem app

### Step 8: Daily Use and Sending

To send crypto:
- Open the app and tap your card to authenticate
- Select the asset, enter recipient address, and amount
- Review details and tap the card again to sign the transaction

All signing happens offline on the card.

## Advanced Tips and Best Practices

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

- Card not detected: Ensure NFC is enabled and tap firmly on the NFC zone (usually upper back of phone)
- App crashes: Force close and restart, or reinstall
- Forgotten access code: Use a backup card to import the wallet (you'll set a new code)
