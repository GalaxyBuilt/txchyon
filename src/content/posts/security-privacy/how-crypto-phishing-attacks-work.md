## Introduction to Crypto Phishing in 2026

Phishing attacks continue to dominate cryptocurrency threats in 2026. These scams evolve rapidly, using AI-generated content, deepfakes, and sophisticated social engineering to steal funds. Losses from phishing exceed hundreds of millions annually, often through direct wallet access rather than traditional credential theft.

Understanding how these attacks operate empowers users to spot and avoid them. This guide covers the most common phishing types active today, real-world mechanisms, and practical prevention steps.

## Why Phishing Thrives in Crypto

Cryptocurrency transactions are irreversible. Once funds transfer, recovery is nearly impossible. Attackers exploit urgency, greed, and trust gaps. Modern tools like generative AI create convincing fakes quickly and at scale.

Common entry points include social media, email, Discord, fake websites, and malicious ads.

## Common Types of Crypto Phishing Attacks in 2026

### 1. Classic Email and Website Phishing

Scammers send emails or links mimicking exchanges, wallets, or support teams. Victims enter seed phrases or connect wallets on fake sites.

**How It Works:**  
Urgent messages claim account issues or rewards. Links lead to cloned sites capturing inputs.

**Prevention:**  
Verify URLs manually. Never enter seed phrases online.

### 2. Wallet Drainers and Malicious Approvals

Drainers are scripts on fake sites prompting wallet connections and unlimited approvals. This allows silent fund transfers later.

**How It Works:**  
Fake airdrops or NFT mints request connections. Signing grants "infinite approval" on tokens.

**Prevention:**  
Use revoke tools regularly—see our [EVM revoke guide](/blog/security-privacy/how-to-revoke-evm). Check approvals before signing.

### 3. Deepfake and AI Voice/Video Scams

AI creates realistic videos or calls from influencers, support, or friends urging urgent actions.

**How It Works:**  
Deepfake videos promote fake giveaways. Voice clones impersonate team members in calls.

**Prevention:**  
Verify requests through official channels. Ignore unsolicited urgent demands.

### 4. Pig Butchering and Romance/Investment Scams

Long-term grooming builds trust, then directs victims to fake platforms.

**How It Works:**  
Relationships form on apps or social media. Victims invest in scammer-controlled sites.

**Prevention:**  
Avoid investment advice from unsolicited contacts. Research platforms independently.

### 5. Fake Airdrops and Giveaway Scams

Promises of free tokens require wallet connections or seed inputs.

**How It Works:**  
Ads or posts mimic legitimate projects. Connections trigger drainers.

**Prevention:**  
Legitimate airdrops never request seeds. Check official announcements.

### 6. Malicious Browser Extensions and Apps

Fake wallet apps or extensions steal keys upon installation.

**How It Works:**  
Impersonate MetaMask or Ledger in stores or links.

**Prevention:**  
Download only from official sources. Verify developer names.

### 7. SMS and Support Impersonation

Texts or chats pose as support, guiding victims to malicious sites.

**How It Works:**  
Claim wallet issues and provide "fix" links.

**Prevention:**  
Official support never initiates contact or requests seeds.

## Real-World Impact and Examples

Phishing caused over $1 billion in losses in recent years. Single drainer incidents drain millions. Pig butchering scams affect thousands emotionally and financially.
