### What Are Bitcoin Ordinals?

Bitcoin Ordinals, launched in 2023 by developer Casey Rodarmor, turn individual satoshis (the smallest unit of Bitcoin: 1 BTC = 100,000,000 sats) into unique digital collectibles.

- **Ordinal Theory**: Assigns a unique serial number to every satoshi based on the order it was mined.
- **Inscriptions**: Attach data (images, text, videos, etc.) directly to a specific satoshi via a Bitcoin transaction. This data lives fully on-chain, making it immutable and secured by Bitcoin's network.

Unlike traditional NFTs on Ethereum (which often store data off-chain), Ordinals are "digital artifacts" with everything embedded in the Bitcoin blockchain.

### What Is BRC-20?

BRC-20 is an experimental fungible token standard on Bitcoin, introduced in 2023 by anonymous developer Domo. It allows creating interchangeable tokens (like ERC-20 on Ethereum) using Ordinal inscriptions.

- **How It Works**: JSON data inscribed on satoshis defines deploy (create token), mint (issue supply), and transfer functions.
- **No Smart Contracts**: Unlike Ethereum tokens, BRC-20 relies on off-chain indexers to track balances.

### The Upgrade: BRC-2.0

In late 2025, BRC-20 evolved into **BRC-2.0**, a major upgrade integrating Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) compatibility directly into its indexer.

- **Key Improvements**: Turns the simple indexer into a Turing-complete virtual machine, enabling smart contracts on Bitcoin-native tokens.
- **Benefits**: Programmable DeFi (lending, swaps), composability with EVM tools, and interaction with Ordinals—all secured by Bitcoin without bridges.
- **Status in 2026**: BRC-2.0 tokens remain dominant in volume, powering more advanced apps while retaining Bitcoin's security.

### What Is the Runes Protocol?

Runes, also created by Casey Rodarmor and launched in 2024 (at Bitcoin's halving), is a UTXO-based fungible token protocol designed as a cleaner alternative to BRC-20.

- **Etching**: Create ("etch") a Rune token by defining its name, supply, and symbol in a transaction.
- **UTXO Model**: Tokens live in unspent outputs, reducing bloat and encouraging responsible management.
- **Efficiency**: Fewer junk UTXOs, lower fees, and simpler than BRC-20.
