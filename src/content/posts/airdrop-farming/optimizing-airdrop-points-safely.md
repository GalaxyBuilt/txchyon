## Introduction

Maximizing airdrop points isn’t about **doing everything everywhere** — it’s about **strategic, efficient, and safe activity**. Overfarming or repeating actions across wallets can trigger Sybil detection and cause points to be reduced or wallets blacklisted.  

This article builds on prior Points Systems cluster articles: [How Crypto Airdrop Points Systems Really Work](/airdrop-farming/airdrop-points-case-studies), [What Actions Actually Earn Airdrop Points](/airdrop-farming/what-actions-earn-airdrop-points), and [How Projects Detect Sybil Wallets](/airdrop-farming/how-projects-detect-sybil-wallets).

## Core Principles of Optimization

1. **Signal Over Noise**  
   - Prioritize meaningful actions over quantity  
   - On-chain + off-chain combined scores outperform pure volume

2. **Timing is Critical**  
   - Avoid burst farming that looks unnatural  
   - Spread activity across days, weeks, or months depending on scoring type

3. **Wallet Hygiene**  
   - Separate wallets logically and operationally  
   - Keep interactions distinct to reduce clustering risk

4. **Tracking & Analysis**  
   - Maintain a spreadsheet or dashboard for points tracking  
   - Identify underperforming wallets or suspicious deductions ([How to Track Airdrop Points Across Multiple Wallets](/airdrop-farming/how-to-track-airdrop-points))

---

## Step-by-Step Optimization Workflow

### Step 1: Identify High-Value Actions
- Prioritize actions that have **historically yielded high points**  
- Focus on staking, LP contributions, governance, and high-quality community engagement

### Step 2: Map Timing Strategy
- Use insights from [Time-Based vs Volume-Based Airdrop Points Systems](/airdrop-farming/time-vs-volume-points-systems)  
- Stagger activities for multiple wallets to avoid identical patterns

### Step 3: Limit Overlap Across Wallets
- Avoid repetitive sequences across wallets  
- Introduce small variations in timing, amount, and action type

### Step 4: Monitor Off-Chain Engagement
- Diversify contributions across Discord, GitHub, testnets, and social channels  
- Ensure each action is unique and authentic

### Step 5: Analyze Results & Adjust
- Track points earned per action  
- Reallocate activity toward actions yielding the best ROI  
- Stop or adjust low-quality activity to reduce risk

## Multi-Wallet Considerations

| Strategy | Why it Matters | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| Separate wallets by campaign | Reduces clustering risk | Assign wallets to specific projects or protocols |
| Stagger activity | Prevents identical patterns | Vary timing and action type |
| Track performance | Identify underperforming wallets | Maintain a centralized log |
| Avoid recycled addresses | Prevents Sybil detection | Use fresh wallets for new campaigns |

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Overfarming**: Too many transactions across wallets in short windows  
- **Automation**: Scripts or bots that perform repetitive actions  
- **Identical Off-Chain Behavior**: Copying posts, comments, or votes across wallets  
- **Ignoring Scoring Type**: Treating all projects as volume-based without checking timing rules  

> Following these rules minimizes risk while maximizing point efficiency.
