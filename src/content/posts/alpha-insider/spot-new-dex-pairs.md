Early DEX arbitrage is one of the fastest ways to capture alpha in crypto trading. The key is spotting **new token pairs** the moment liquidity is added and executing trades **before bots and public attention** create price inefficiencies.

## Step 1: Monitor New Pair Listings

- Use **on-chain scanners and alert bots** to track new liquidity events on DEXs like Uniswap, PancakeSwap, or Raydium.  
- Focus on **pairs with low initial volume**, which are most likely to have large early price swings.  
- Consider **linking to wallet monitoring techniques** for multi-wallet setups: [High-Frequency Airdrop Tactics](/blog/alpha-insider/high-frequency-airdrops).

---

## Step 2: Analyze Price and Liquidity Depth

- Check liquidity pool size — **smaller pools move faster** and create alpha opportunities.  
- Examine token distribution: major deployer wallets signal **high volatility potential**.  
- Review **slippage risk** to plan your entry and exit carefully.  

> Related: Learn how insiders leverage deployer patterns in [Smart Contract Sniping](/blog/alpha-insider/smart-contract-sniping).

---

## Step 3: Execution Timing

- Use **fast wallets** with gas priority or mempool monitoring for immediate execution.  
- Decide between **manual vs automated execution** depending on risk tolerance.  
- Front-run carefully — anti-bot systems are increasingly sophisticated.  

> Complementary article: [Cross-Chain Early Arbitrage](/blog/alpha-insider/cross-chain-early-arbitrage) explores timing and execution when trading across multiple chains.  

---

## Step 4: Risk Management

- Only allocate a **small portion of capital per pair**; early trades carry high risk.  
- Monitor price movement constantly and exit before volatility stabilizes.  
- Avoid investing in projects with **suspicious contracts** or unknown deployer history.  

> For exit strategies, see [Token Launch & Fair Launch Tactics](/blog/alpha-insider/token-launch-fair-launch-tactics).
